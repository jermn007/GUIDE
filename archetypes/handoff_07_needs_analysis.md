# Archetype 7: Needs Analysis & Front-End Design

**Purpose**: Evaluate needs assessments, learner analyses, goal analyses, performance gap analyses, and instructional goals for completeness, rigor, and evidence-based decision-making.

**Evaluation Context**: Use this archetype when assessing:
- Performance gap analyses from organizational requests
- Instructional goals linked to documented needs
- Learner/audience analyses for course design
- Business impact analyses leading to training decisions
- HPT (Human Performance Technology) cause analyses
- Needs assessment reports from external consultants
- Mager-format instructional objectives
- Stakeholder alignment documentation for training projects

---

## Rubric: 6 Dimensions (1-5 Scale)

### 1. Performance Gap Identification (1-5)

Is the gap between desired and actual performance clearly defined and DATA-BASED (not anecdotal)?

| Score | Gap Definition | Data-Based | Quantified | Root Cause | Scope |
|-------|---------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **1** | Vague or implied | Anecdotal only ("people struggle") | No numbers | Not identified | Unclear |
| **2** | Described but fuzzy | Some data; mostly anecdotal | Rough estimate | Implied | Narrow view |
| **3** | Moderately clear | Mix of data and anecdote | Some quantification | Partially identified | Reasonable scope |
| **4** | Clear definition | Data-driven; some anecdote | Mostly quantified (e.g., "30% of X fail task Y") | Identified | Comprehensive |
| **5** | Explicit, measurable gap | Fully data-based (metrics, surveys, observations) | Precisely quantified (e.g., "Performance gap: Current 65%, Desired 85%") | Root cause named | Full context |

**Guidance:**
- **Data types**: Learning outcomes data, performance metrics, completion rates, error rates, stakeholder surveys, job analysis data, business metrics
- **Avoid**: "People need training" without data; "This would be nice to have" (not a gap)
- **Sweet spot**: "Current state: 65% of new employees can execute Process X without assistance. Desired state: 95% within 30 days. Gap: 30 percentage points."

### 2. Cause Analysis (1-5)

Are root causes identified BEFORE jumping to solutions? Does analysis distinguish environmental barriers from skill/behavior gaps?

| Score | Cause Analysis | Environmental vs. Behavior | Non-Training Intervention | Training Appropriateness | Documentation |
|-------|---------------|---------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| **1** | None; assumes training | Not distinguished | None identified | Assumes training default | None |
| **2** | Superficial; mostly behavior | Mentioned but not separated | Maybe one non-training option | Training may be wrong | Minimal |
| **3** | Moderate analysis | Partially distinguished | One or two non-training options | Training considered appropriate | Documented |
| **4** | Good cause analysis | Clearly distinguished | Multiple options explored | Training justified if behavior gap | Clear documentation |
| **5** | Comprehensive cause analysis | Fully distinguished (HPT model) | All 7 intervention categories explored | Training recommended ONLY if behavior gap identified | Detailed cause-solution map |

**HPT Model: Environmental Barriers vs. Behavior Gaps (Van Tiem et al., ISPI)**

**Environmental Support** (Training will NOT fix these):
- **Tools/Resources**: Equipment missing, software unavailable, job aids absent
- **Feedback/Feedback**: Unclear expectations, no performance feedback, wrong incentives
- **Incentives**: Misaligned rewards (e.g., fast but wrong is rewarded more than slow but right)
- **Process/Job Design**: Workflow inefficient, role unclear, unclear authority
- **Organizational Support**: Conflicting priorities, unclear vision, lack of buy-in from leadership

**Behavior Gaps** (Training CAN help):
- **Skill Gaps**: Employees don't know HOW to do something (procedural, technical)
- **Knowledge Gaps**: Employees don't know WHAT or WHY (conceptual understanding)
- **Attitude/Motivation Gaps**: Employees don't WANT to do something (confidence, attitude)
- **Capacity Gaps**: Employees lack physical/cognitive capacity (rare; usually misdiagnosed)

**HPT Seven Intervention Categories (Van Tiem et al., 2000):**
1. **Performance Support**: Job aids, checklists, reference materials, software support
2. **Job Design**: Workflow redesign, task simplification, role clarity
3. **Personal Development**: Training, coaching, mentoring, certification
4. **HR Systems**: Hiring criteria, promotion standards, compensation, benefits
5. **Organizational Communications**: Clarity of vision, change communication, feedback channels
6. **Organizational Design**: Structure, governance, accountability, decision authority
7. **Financial/Incentive Systems**: Compensation alignment, incentives, bonus structure

**Critical Insight**: If the gap is environmental, training is waste. Example:
- **Stated Gap**: "Sales team closing rate dropped 15%"
- **Bad Analysis**: "Sales team needs better negotiation skills training"
- **Good Analysis**: "Root causes: (1) CRM system change created 30% time overhead (environmental), (2) Commission structure changed, reducing urgency (incentive misalignment), (3) 20% of team never got onboarding (training need). Solution: Fix CRM workflow, realign commissions, provide training to onboarded group."

### 3. Needs Assessment Completeness (1-5)

Are all four levels addressed? (McGoldrick & Tobey model; each builds on previous)

| Score | Business Needs | Performance Needs | Learning Needs | Learner Needs | Connection |
|-------|---------------|------------------|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| **1** | Not addressed | Not addressed | Assumed | Minimal | Disconnected |
| **2** | Mentioned vaguely | Vague ("improve performance") | Mentioned | Basic demographics | Weak connection |
| **3** | Outlined | Described moderately | Outlined | Described | Moderate connection |
| **4** | Clear description | Well-described; linked to business | Clear; linked to performance | Well-documented | Clear hierarchy |
| **5** | Strategic alignment clear | Explicit performance targets; linked to business | Specific learning outcomes; linked to performance | Comprehensive profile; preferred learning styles, constraints | Each level builds logically on previous |

**McGoldrick & Tobey Four-Level Model:**

| Level | Question | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **1: Business Needs** | What does the organization need to achieve? What's the strategic imperative? | "Increase customer retention from 85% to 92% within 18 months to improve lifetime value by $500K" |
| **2: Performance Needs** | What must people DO differently to achieve business goal? What performance change is required? | "Customer service reps must resolve 85% of issues in first contact (vs. current 60%) and follow new retention protocol" |
| **3: Learning Needs** | What must people KNOW/LEARN to perform differently? | "Reps must know: (1) New retention protocol steps, (2) How to recognize high-churn customer signals, (3) Empathy de-escalation techniques" |
| **4: Learner Needs** | Who are they? What are their characteristics, constraints, and preferences? | "500 reps; current avg. tenure 3 years; 70% remote; 40% non-native English; prefer short videos; morning availability only" |

**Connection Example:**
- Business (L1) → Performance (L2): "To achieve retention target, reps must close more issues first-contact"
- Performance (L2) → Learning (L3): "To close issues first-contact, reps must know X protocol and Y techniques"
- Learning (L3) → Learner (L4): "Teach X and Y via short videos (remote-friendly) in morning (availability constraint) with translations (language consideration)"

### 4. Goal & Objective Quality (1-5)

Do goals link directly to documented performance gaps? Are objectives written to Mager standard?

| Score | Link to Gap | Mager Format (B+C+Cr) | Feasibility Screening | Learning-Job Match | Clarity |
|-------|------------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|---------|
| **1** | No link; invented goals | Missing 2+ components | Not screened | No match | Vague |
| **2** | Weak link | Partial Mager format | Minimal screening | Weak match | Somewhat clear |
| **3** | Moderate link | Most components; one weak | Basic screening | Moderate match | Clear |
| **4** | Good link | All Mager components; one minor gap | Good screening (resources, content stability) | Good match | Very clear |
| **5** | Direct link; traceable to data | All Mager components; precise | Comprehensive screening; stakeholder sign-off | Perfect match (learning transfers to job) | Crystal clear |

**Mager's Standard for Objectives:**
**[Behavior] [Condition] [Criterion]**

- **Behavior**: Observable, measurable action (verb: "execute," "identify," "troubleshoot," "select" - NOT "understand," "learn," "know")
- **Condition**: Context in which behavior occurs (e.g., "given a customer complaint," "using the CRM system," "in a group discussion")
- **Criterion**: Standard for acceptable performance (e.g., "within 3 minutes," "with 90% accuracy," "without referencing notes")

**Mager Examples:**

| Poor | Better (Mager) |
|------|--|
| "Understand customer service" | "Given a customer complaint via email, the rep will respond with a solution or escalation within 2 hours, following the protocol, with 95% accuracy as rated by supervisor" |
| "Learn the new system" | "Using the new CRM, the rep will execute a customer inquiry lookup, retrieve account history, and generate a follow-up task, all without error and within 5 minutes" |
| "Know retention techniques" | "When presented with a high-risk customer scenario, the rep will select and apply the appropriate de-escalation technique from the training, resulting in customer agreement to stay (as measured by 1-month follow-up)" |

**Feasibility Screening (Mager, 1997):**
- Can we teach it? (Is content stable? Can we find/create materials?)
- Do we have resources? (Budget, facilitators, technology)
- Will learners be available? (Realistic time commitment for their role)
- Will it transfer to job? (Transfer context available; manager support; job design allows application)
- Is it measurable? (Can we assess if objective met post-training?)

### 5. Stakeholder Alignment (1-5)

Are decision-makers involved? Is there documented buy-in? Are findings clearly separated from recommendations?

| Score | Stakeholder Involvement | Buy-In Documentation | Findings vs. Recommendations | Conflict Resolution | Communication |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **1** | None; done in isolation | None | Mixed together | None; conflicts hidden | Not communicated |
| **2** | Minimal; weak involvement | Vague agreement | Somewhat separated | Mentioned only | Minimal communication |
| **3** | Moderate involvement | Basic agreement noted | Mostly separated | Addressed | Communicated to core team |
| **4** | Good involvement; multiple stakeholders | Formal sign-off | Clearly separated | Documented | Shared with full group |
| **5** | Comprehensive (sponsor, SME, learner rep, manager) | Explicit signatures; agreement on priorities | Objective data vs. recommendations clearly demarcated | All conflicts addressed and resolved | Stakeholders briefed; consensus documented |

**Key Stakeholders to Involve:**
- **Sponsor** (Budget holder, strategic owner): Approves training decision, business case
- **Subject Matter Expert (SME)**: Validates performance gap, content accuracy
- **Learner Representatives**: Represent target audience; validate feasibility and relevance
- **Manager/Supervisor**: Will implement on job; must support transfer
- **HR/Operations**: Implementation logistics, scheduling, compliance

**Data vs. Recommendations Example:**
```
FINDINGS (Objective Data):
- Survey: 75% of reps report confusion with new CRM
- Completion data: Only 50% completed onboarding in past 2 months
- Performance: First-contact resolution dropped 8 percentage points post-implementation
- Root cause analysis: 60% of issues are CRM navigation; 30% are process confusion; 10% are knowledge gaps

RECOMMENDATIONS (Your interpretation):
1. CRITICAL (Environment): Redesign CRM workflow to reduce navigation steps (estimated 4 weeks, $15K)
2. HIGH (Process): Create role-specific job aid cards (1 week, $2K)
3. MEDIUM (Training): Provide targeted training on process changes to 20% of reps who missed onboarding (2 days, $1K)
4. LOW (Incentive): Add navigation efficiency to bonus metrics for next quarter

Rationale: 90% of gap is environmental/process; only 10% is knowledge gap. Training alone will not close gap.
```

### 6. Intervention Appropriateness (1-5)

Is the recommended intervention matched to the identified cause? Is training the right solution or a default?

| Score | Cause-Match | Intervention Category | Training Justification | Non-Training Options | Evidence |
|-------|------------|----------------------|----------------------|----------------------|----------|
| **1** | Wrong; training default | Training only (wrong) | None; assumed | None explored | Anecdotal |
| **2** | Partial match | Training primary (maybe wrong) | Weak justification | One option mentioned | Some evidence |
| **3** | Moderate match | Training + one other | Reasonable justification | Two or three options explored | Decent evidence |
| **4** | Good match | Multiple interventions; training justified | Clear justification if training included | Four or five options explored | Strong evidence |
| **5** | Perfect match | Intervention mix matched to causes (HPT 7 categories) | Training recommended ONLY if behavior gap proven | All 7 categories considered; justified exclusion | Comprehensive cause-intervention map |

**Intervention Selection Guide (Van Tiem et al.):**

| Identified Cause | Appropriate Interventions | Training? |
|-----------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **Skills/knowledge gap** | Personal development (training, coaching) + performance support (job aids) | YES |
| **Motivation/confidence gap** | Personal development (mentoring, coaching) + incentive alignment | Sometimes |
| **Unclear expectations** | Organizational communication (clarity) + job design (role specification) | NO |
| **Missing tools/resources** | Job design (process redesign) + performance support (tools, technology) | NO |
| **Misaligned incentives** | Financial/incentive systems (compensation redesign) | NO |
| **Process inefficiency** | Job design (workflow redesign) + performance support (automation, tools) | NO |
| **Organizational barriers** | Org design (structure, authority clarification) + org communication | NO |
| **Staffing gaps** | HR systems (hiring, promotion) + personal development (training for new hires) | Maybe |

**Red Flag: "Training is Recommended But..." (Anti-patterns)**
- "We need training because morale is low" (Morale issue, not skill issue; try incentives, communication)
- "Let's do training to fix the process" (Process issue; design job better, don't bandaid with training)
- "Training will make people want to use the new system" (System design issue; fix the interface, not the person)
- "We'll train everyone on the new policy" (Clear expectations issue; communicate policy, maybe train on procedures IF there are skills)

## System Prompt (Judge Rubric)

```
You are an expert in instructional design needs analysis, front-end analysis, and performance improvement.
Your role is to evaluate needs assessments and instructional goals for completeness and appropriateness.

You will score across 6 dimensions, each on a 1-5 scale:

1. **Performance Gap Identification** (1-5)
   - Gap between desired and actual performance clearly defined and DATA-BASED (not anecdotal)
   - HPT Model (Van Tiem et al.): Environmental support barriers distinguished from behavior/skill gaps
   - Gap quantified: specific metrics, measurements, or evidence provided
   - Root cause identified: not just symptom description
   Score: 1=Vague, anecdotal, no data; 5=Clear, quantified, data-driven gap definition

2. **Cause Analysis** (1-5)
   - Root causes identified BEFORE jumping to solutions
   - HPT key distinction: Environmental barriers (tools, expectations, incentives, feedback, resources, processes, consequences) vs. Repertory of behavior gaps (skills, knowledge, attitudes, capacity)
   - Training appropriate ONLY for skill/knowledge/attitude gaps, not environmental barriers
   - Non-training solutions identified where appropriate (job redesign, tool provision, process improvement, incentive alignment)
   Score: 1=No cause analysis, assumes training default; 5=Comprehensive cause analysis, environmental vs. behavior distinguished

3. **Needs Assessment Completeness** (1-5)
   McGoldrick & Tobey four-level model (each builds on previous):
   - Level 1 (Business needs): Organizational goals, strategic priorities, business outcomes
   - Level 2 (Performance needs): What must people do differently to achieve business goals?
   - Level 3 (Learning needs): What must people learn to perform differently?
   - Level 4 (Learner needs): Characteristics, preferences, constraints of target learners
   Score: 1=Only one level assessed; 5=All four levels addressed with clear connections

4. **Goal & Objective Quality** (1-5)
   - Goals linked directly to documented performance gaps (not invented)
   - Objectives written to Mager standard: Behavior (observable action) + Condition (context) + Criterion (standard)
   - Goals screened for feasibility: resources available, content stable, learner availability realistic
   - Learning outcomes match performance requirements (no gap between learning and job application)
   Score: 1=Vague goals, not Mager-formatted, disconnected from gap; 5=Clear Mager objectives linked to gaps

5. **Stakeholder Alignment** (1-5)
   - Key decision-makers involved in assessment: sponsor, SME, learner representatives, manager
   - Documented buy-in: stakeholder agreement on findings and priorities
   - Findings clearly separated from recommendations: data presented objectively
   - Conflicting stakeholder perspectives acknowledged
   Score: 1=No stakeholder input; 5=Multiple stakeholders engaged with documented agreement

6. **Intervention Appropriateness** (1-5)
   HPT Seven Intervention Categories (Van Tiem):
   - Performance support (job aids, tools, checklists)
   - Job design (workflow, role clarity, task structure)
   - Personal development (training, coaching, mentoring)
   - HR systems (hiring, promotion, compensation, incentives)
   - Organizational communications (clarity, feedback, transparency)
   - Organizational design (structure, accountability, authority)
   - Financial/incentive systems (aligned with desired performance)

   Is recommended intervention matched to identified cause? Training appropriate only if learning gap identified.
   Score: 1=Wrong intervention for cause (e.g., training for environmental barrier); 5=Intervention precisely matched to root cause

Return only a valid JSON object with keys: performance_gap_identification, cause_analysis, needs_assessment_completeness, goal_objective_quality, stakeholder_alignment, intervention_appropriateness.
Each key should map to an integer 1-5.
Include a "summary" key with 1-2 sentences on overall assessment quality and critical gaps.
```

### Expected Scoring Patterns
- **Rigorous assessment (5-4)**: All 4 levels addressed; data-based gap; cause analysis distinguishes environment/behavior; Mager objectives linked to gap; multiple intervention options explored; training recommended only if learning gap; full stakeholder alignment.
- **Adequate assessment (3)**: Moderate completeness; some data; cause analysis present; objectives somewhat linked; one or two interventions; reasonable stakeholder involvement.
- **Weak assessment (2)**: One or two levels only; mostly anecdotal; cause analysis missing or superficial; vague objectives; training assumed default; limited stakeholder input.
- **Non-starter (1)**: Jumps straight to solution (usually training); no needs data; no cause analysis; vague goals; training default; no stakeholder alignment.

## Common Evaluation Scenarios

### Scenario A: Sales Team Performance Drop
**Request**: "Sales closing rate dropped 15% in Q2. We need training to improve negotiation skills."

**Rigorous Needs Assessment Would Include:**
- **L1 (Business)**: Revenue impact if closing rate not improved; strategic importance of this segment
- **L2 (Performance)**: Current closing rate 45%, target 60%; what reps must do differently (more discovery questions? better objection handling? faster follow-up?)
- **L3 (Learning)**: If closing gap is skill-based: negotiation tactics, objection handling, discovery process knowledge
- **Cause Analysis**: Is it skills? Or is it the new CRM adding 30% overhead? Or commission change reducing motivation? Or competitor's new offering?
- **L4 (Learner)**: 20 reps; mix of tenured and new; time-zone constraints; prefer short, on-demand content
- **Intervention**: If cause = CRM overhead → fix CRM workflow + maybe training. If cause = incentive → realign commissions + maybe skills coaching. If cause = knowledge gap → training (with job aids). Don't assume training.

**Poor Assessment**: "We need a 2-day negotiation skills workshop" (assumes training; no data; wrong intervention if root cause is process or incentives)

---

### Scenario B: LMS Adoption Resistance
**Request**: "New LMS launched 4 weeks ago; only 30% of required courses completed. Employees report confusion. We need training on how to use the system."

**Rigorous Needs Assessment Would Include:**
- **L1 (Business)**: Compliance requirement, deadline, business impact of non-completion
- **L2 (Performance)**: What must employees DO with the LMS (enroll, launch course, track completion, access mobile)?
- **Cause Analysis**: Is it:
  - **Environmental** (LMS interface confusing, unclear instructions, poor navigation) → job design (redesign interface, clearer instructions, help text)?
  - **Knowledge** (employees don't know how to enroll) → training (brief demo, job aid)?
  - **Incentive** (no urgency since deadline is 3 months away) → communication (emphasize deadline)?
  - **Resistance** (change fatigue from recent system migrations) → communication (why new system, benefits)?
- **Appropriate Solution Mix**: Likely environment + communication issue, not training issue. Training alone on "how to click buttons" won't help if the interface is confusing.

**Poor Assessment**: "We need LMS training for all 500 employees" (assumes training; likely the real problem is interface design or communication, not knowledge)

---

### Scenario C: New Process Implementation
**Request**: "We're implementing a new compliance process next month. Develop training to help employees learn it."

**Rigorous Needs Assessment Would Include:**
- **L1 (Business)**: Why implement? Compliance requirement? Efficiency? Risk reduction?
- **L2 (Performance)**: What must employees DO differently? Which roles affected? Current process vs. new process?
- **L3 (Learning)**: What's new or different in the process? What knowledge required? Procedural steps? Why the change? Decision criteria?
- **L4 (Learner)**: Which departments? How many employees? Technical literacy? Time available? Learning preferences?
- **Process Analysis**: Is process design clear? Are there ambiguities? Do employees have tools (checklists, job aids) to execute?
- **Appropriate Solution Mix**: Likely training (process knowledge) + job design (clarity, tools) + communication (why change). Separate "what's new" from "how to learn."

**Poor Assessment**: "We'll create a 1-hour training module covering the entire new process" (doesn't assess whether process is clear; doesn't include job aids; assumes employees will retain everything from 1 hour)

## Validation Checklist

Use this before scoring:

- [ ] Business need explicitly stated with strategic connection
- [ ] Performance gap quantified with data (metrics, not anecdotes)
- [ ] Root cause analysis distinguishes environmental barriers from behavior gaps
- [ ] All four levels (business, performance, learning, learner) addressed
- [ ] Goals linked directly to documented performance gap
- [ ] Learning objectives written in Mager format (behavior + condition + criterion)
- [ ] Objectives feasible (resources available, content stable, time realistic)
- [ ] Multiple stakeholders involved (sponsor, SME, learner rep, manager)
- [ ] Stakeholder buy-in documented (signatures or explicit agreement)
- [ ] Findings separated from recommendations (data vs. interpretation)
- [ ] Intervention(s) matched to root cause(s), not default to training
- [ ] Non-training interventions explored and justified (why or why not included)

## Score Interpretation for Stakeholders

| Overall Range | Status | Action |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| **4.5-5.0** | Excellent | Evidence-based, multi-level assessment; high confidence in intervention match; proceed |
| **3.5-4.4** | Good | Solid assessment; moderate gaps; appropriate intervention; minor refinements may help |
| **2.5-3.4** | Fair | Assessment incomplete; major gaps in cause analysis or stakeholder alignment; supplement before action |
| **1.5-2.4** | Poor | Significant gaps; likely wrong intervention selected; reassess before proceeding |
| **<1.5** | Inadequate | Cannot proceed; assessment requires substantial rework; high risk of wrong training decision |

## Quick Filter: Is This a Training Issue? (Decision Tree)

1. **Is there a documented performance gap?**
   - No → Not a training issue; stop here
   - Yes → Continue

2. **Is the gap caused by lack of skill/knowledge/confidence (behavior)?**
   - No → Not a training issue; consider other interventions (job design, process, incentives, org design, communication)
   - Yes → Training may be appropriate; continue

3. **Are environmental barriers present (unclear expectations, missing tools, misaligned incentives, poor process)?**
   - Yes → Fix environment FIRST/SIMULTANEOUSLY with training; training alone insufficient
   - No → Training likely sufficient

4. **Will employees have opportunity to apply learning on the job?**
   - No → Training will not transfer; reconsider intervention
   - Yes → Training likely effective

5. **Are learners available and motivated to learn?**
   - No → Address motivation/availability before training
   - Yes → Proceed with training + supports

**If you answer "No" to questions 1, 2, or 4, training is probably the wrong solution.**

## Red Flags: Signs of Poor Needs Analysis

- "We've decided to do training; design a course on [topic]" (No needs assessment; cart before horse)
- "Training will fix [environmental problem]" (Wrong intervention)
- "All employees need training on [policy]" (Probably just communication issue; not learning gap)
- "We need a 2-day workshop to train on the new system" (No assessment of what employees actually need to learn)
- "This training will improve employee engagement" (Engagement issue, not learning issue)
- "Everyone's struggling, so let's add training" (Without cause analysis, training is a band-aid)
- Goals like "understand," "learn," "be familiar with" (Not behavioral; not measurable)
- No mention of job transfer or application context (How will learning transfer to job?)
- No stakeholder buy-in documented (Risky if sponsor or manager not aligned)

If you see these, score lower and recommend reassessment.
