# GUIDE Rubric - Self-Evaluation Comparison Report
## v2.2.0 Baseline vs. v3.0.0 Post-Improvement

**Date:** April 12, 2026
**Method:** Each applicable archetype's scoring criteria applied to the rubric document itself
**Purpose:** Measure whether v3.0.0 improvements moved the needle from the v2.2.0 baseline (composite 3.4/5.0)

---

## What Changed in v3.0.0

The following sections were added or revised based on the v2.2.0 self-evaluation findings:

1. **Reader Objectives** - Six explicit "After reading this document, you will be able to..." statements
2. **Why Nine Archetypes? Domain Coverage Rationale** - ADDIE lifecycle mapping explaining archetype selection
3. **How to Use This Document** - Six-step guided workflow from archetype selection through action
4. **Calibration Example: Scoring in Practice** - Full worked example with sample artifact, scored dimensions, and calibration check callout
5. **Sharpened Mid-Range Criteria** - All Archetype 01 Score 3 and Score 4 descriptors replaced vague language ("mostly," "minor lapses") with concrete, countable indicators

## SUMMARY

The v3.0.0 improvements raised the composite score from **3.4 to 4.0** (+0.6 points, +18%). The two formerly critical archetypes (Assessment Design and Instructional Sequencing) each improved by 1.0+ points and moved from HIGH priority to LOW. Cognitive Neuroscience improved meaningfully (+0.7) and moved from HIGH to MEDIUM.

The single highest-impact addition was the **Calibration Example**, which simultaneously addressed inter-rater reliability (Arch 02), scaffolding and practice (Arch 03), and episodic memory engagement (Arch 09). This confirms the v2.2.0 self-evaluation's prediction that a worked example would be the highest-leverage improvement.

The remaining gaps are primarily in multimedia/visual representation and deeper practice opportunities - areas that would benefit from format expansion beyond a static Word document.
